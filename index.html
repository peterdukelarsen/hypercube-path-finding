MATLAB 4D Tessaract Visualization

This is a video of the MATLAB visualization we used to understand how the 4D visualization of hypercubes works. The program works by generating a 4D cube's matrix, and plotting all of the x and y coordinates of it (program ignores projections - though they are explained in our presentation). This method ignores projections in higher dimensions, and instead seeks to create a proper visualization through rotations. The program combines all of the rotation matrices for 4 dimensions, and then rotates them simultaneously by a singele angle value (theta). This then gives a rather complete appearance of the 4D visualization, though it does miss out on a few images because of its lack of projection. The writer of the code mentions in their paper that the projections were too complex for the scope of their project, and was a rather tough concept to grasp in 4 dimensions.
